The Cuban Missile Crisis was a 13-day stand-off between the United States and the Soviet Union in 1962 over the placement of nuclear missiles in Cuba. The Soviet Union had agreed to place missiles on Cuban soil with the understanding that they would counter any U.S. missile threats to the Soviet Union while ensuring Cuba's security.
