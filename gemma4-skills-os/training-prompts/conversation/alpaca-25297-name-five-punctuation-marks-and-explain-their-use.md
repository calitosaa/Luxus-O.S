## Asistente
The five main punctuation marks are the period (.), the comma (,), the semicolon (;), the colon (:), and the apostrophe (’). 

The period is used to denote the end of a sentence. 

The comma is used to separate parts of a sentence, or to denote a pause.

The semicolon is used to separate two main clauses in a sentence, or to join two closely related sentences. 

The colon is used to introduce a list, a quotation, or an explanation of a preceding idea. 

The apostrophe is used to denote possession, to indicate missing letters, or to form a contraction.
