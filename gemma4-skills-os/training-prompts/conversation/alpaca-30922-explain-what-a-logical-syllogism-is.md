Explain what a logical syllogism is.

## Asistente
A logical syllogism is a three-part argument used in logic to arrive at a conclusion. It consists of two premises and one conclusion. The first premise states a universal truth, the second premise narrows that truth and the conclusion is derived from combining the two premises. For example, the syllogism “all humans are mortal, Socrates is a human, therefore Socrates is mortal” relies on the universal truth that “all humans are mortal”, and provides proof by showing that Socrates is a human. Therefore, the conclusion that Socrates is mortal follows logically.
