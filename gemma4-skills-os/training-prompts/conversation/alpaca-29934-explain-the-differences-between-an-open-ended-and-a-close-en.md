## Usuario
Explain the differences between an open-ended and a close-ended question.

## Asistente
Open-ended questions do not have a fixed answer, allowing the respondent to provide any kind of answer. These questions usually begin with words such as “what,” “how,” “why,” or “describe”. They can generate a lot of valuable insights. On the other hand, close-ended questions can be answered with a simple “yes” or “no”, or with a fixed selection of responses. These questions often begin with “do”, “are”, or “would”. They are useful for collecting quantitative data, but not for gathering qualitative information.
