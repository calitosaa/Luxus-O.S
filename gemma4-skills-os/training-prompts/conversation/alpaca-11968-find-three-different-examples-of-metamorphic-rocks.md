## Asistente
Examples of metamorphic rocks include slate, gneiss, schist, and marble. 

Slate is a fine-grained metamorphic rock usually composed of clay or volcanic ash which can be split into thin, even layers. 

Gneiss is a medium to coarse-grained metamorphic rock composed of alternating bands composed of quartz, mica, and feldspar. 

Schist is a medium to coarse-grained, banded metamorphic rock composed largely of quartz and mica minerals. 

Marble is a fine-grained, crystalline, metamorphic rock composed mainly of recrystallized calcite or dolomite.
