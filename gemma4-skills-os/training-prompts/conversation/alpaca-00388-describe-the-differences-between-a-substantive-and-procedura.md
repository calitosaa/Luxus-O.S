Describe the differences between a substantive and procedural law.

## Asistente
Substantive laws establish rules and regulations governing behavior and conduct, while procedural laws determine how the substantive laws will be enforced. Substantive law defines and regulates rights and duties, and typically consists of criminal, civil, and administrative laws. Procedural law focuses on how the legal system will handle disputes that arise under the substantive law by outlining the steps, rules, and procedures of civil and criminal courts.
