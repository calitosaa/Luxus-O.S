Inferential statistics is a type of statistical analysis used to draw conclusions about a population based on a sample. It is used to make predictions and generalizations about a larger group of individuals, known as a population, on the basis of information obtained from a smaller group of individuals, known as a sample. The results of inferential statistics are subject to variability and can be affected by factors such as sample size, population variability, and the nature of the statistical test used.
