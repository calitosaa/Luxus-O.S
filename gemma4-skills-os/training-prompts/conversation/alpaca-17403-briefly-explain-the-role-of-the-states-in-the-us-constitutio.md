## Asistente
The US Constitution divides the power of the federal government between the states and the federal government. States are given the power to handle matters that are considered “internally” related to the state, while the federal government handles matters considered “externally” related or that affect the country as a whole. This means that the federal government has control over issues such as foreign policy and currency, while the states have control over issues such as education and health care. The states also have the right to ratify constitutional amendments, which gives them a powerful role in the federal government.
