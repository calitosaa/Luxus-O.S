Aspirin is a widely used and widely available over-the-counter pain reliever. It is most commonly used to relieve mild-to-moderate pain, reduce fever, and relieve inflammation. It is also used to prevent heart attacks and strokes. 

The most common side effects of aspirin include stomach irritation, indigestion, nausea and vomiting. Less common but more severe side effects include bleeding, heartburn and allergic reactions. Aspirin can also cause constipation, diarrhea and drowsiness. 

In terms of effectiveness, aspirin is generally considered to be an effective pain reliever and anti-inflammatory agent. In some cases, aspirin may also be used as a preventative treatment for heart attacks and strokes. Clinical studies have suggested that aspirin can reduce the risk of heart attack and stroke in some patients.
