## Asistente
Natural language processing (NLP) and deep learning are two closely related fields of Artificial Intelligence. NLP refers to computer programs that are able to interpret and analyze natural language, while deep learning focuses on the development of algorithms that can learn patterns from large amounts of data. 

The primary difference between NLP and deep learning is the type of data they use. NLP uses human language as its input, while deep learning typically uses numerical data. In NLP, the algorithms must maintain a strong understanding of the language’s grammar and syntax in order to interpret the meaning of the text, while deep learning algorithms can simply look for patterns in the data. Furthermore, deep learning algorithms are able to take into account multiple factors and generate more accurate results than NLP.
