## Asistente
Supply-Side Economics: Supply-side economics is an economic theory that focuses on increasing the productivity of the economy through incentives such as tax cuts. It has its roots in classical economics and sees supply as the main driver of economic growth. It believes that increased supply will lead to increased demand and thus drive economic growth. 

Keynesian Economics: Keynesian economics is an economic theory that emphasizes the role of government in managing and stimulating the economy. It sees government spending as a tool that can be used to regulate economic growth, leading to increased employment and consumption. It believes that demand is the primary driver of economic growth.
