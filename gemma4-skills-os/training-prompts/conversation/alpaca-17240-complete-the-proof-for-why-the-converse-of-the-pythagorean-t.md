## Usuario
Complete the proof for why the converse of the Pythagorean Theorem is false.

## Asistente
Suppose we let A, B, and C represent the sides of a triangle such that C is the longest side and A² + B² = C². This is the Pythagorean Theorem. If we assume the converse, namely that A² + B² = C² implies C is the longest side, then there is a possibility of a triangle where A > C or B > C. This contradicts the original assumption that C is the longest side of the triangle, therefore proving that the converse of the Pythagorean Theorem is false.
