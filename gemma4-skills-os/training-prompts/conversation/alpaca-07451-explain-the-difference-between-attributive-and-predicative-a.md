## Asistente
Attributive adjectives are adjectives that appear before the noun or pronoun that they modify. An example of this type of adjective is "bad" in the phrase "bad cat". Predicative adjectives, on the other hand, appear after the noun or pronoun they modify. An example of this type of adjective is "tired" in the phrase "the cat is tired".
