## Usuario
Compare and contrast two potential methods of customer segmentation.

Contexto/Input:
Demographic segmentation and psychographic segmentation.

## Asistente
Demographic segmentation is based on customer characteristics such as age, gender, occupation, income, education, and marital status. This segmentation technique can be used to tailor a product or service to the needs of the customer by closely examining customer data that is collected through surveys and market research. 

In contrast, psychographic segmentation is based on customer lifestyle and personality. This approach is used to determine the interests and values of a customer, as well as their buying habits, interests, and lifestyle. Psychographic segmentation helps companies to identify certain customer groups with certain values, beliefs, and interests that they can appeal to with their products or services. 

Both demographic and psychographic segmentation are useful methods to categorize customers, however they each have different advantages. Demographic segmentation can be done quickly and easily, which is why it is commonly used and often the starting point for a market segmentation. Psychographic segmentation can be more helpful to differentiate and define target markets, as it reveals lifestyle and values which are more closely associated with attitudes and behaviour. Ultimately, a combination of the two segmentation methods can provide a more accurate picture of a customer’s characteristics and needs, enabling companies to better target their marketing strategies and deliver better products and services to their customers.
