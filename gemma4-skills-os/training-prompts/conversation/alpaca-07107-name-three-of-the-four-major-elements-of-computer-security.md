## Asistente
The four major elements of computer security are confidentiality, integrity, availability, and authentication. Confidentiality means that only authorized users should have access to sensitive data; integrity ensures that data is accurate and unchanged; availability means that data is always accessible; and authentication is a way of verifying the identity of users.
