## Asistente
The five most influential figures in the history of artificial intelligence are Alan Turing, Marvin Minsky, John McCarthy, Arthur Samuel, and Geoffrey Hinton. Alan Turing invented the Turing Machine, the first model of a universal computing machine, and formed the basis for the "Turing Test" for artificial intelligence. Marvin Minsky founded the Artificial Intelligence Laboratory at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, was an early pioneer of robotics, and proposed the idea of artificial neural networks. John McCarthy is often referred to as the Father of Artificial Intelligence, and his research focuses on programming languages, game theory, and the use of context in decision-making. Arthur Samuel pioneered the concept of machine learning and developed the first computer program to learn. Finally, Geoffrey Hinton was an important contributor to the development of deep learning and neural networks.
