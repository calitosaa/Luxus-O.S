## Asistente
Black-box testing is a testing method in which the inner workings of the system being tested are unknown to the tester. The goal of black-box testing is to assess the functionality of the system based on its specified requirements. This process involves the tester inputting various scenarios into the system and checking the system’s output for accuracy. Black-box testing is often used to identify issues such as bugs and usability problems.
