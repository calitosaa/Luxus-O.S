## Usuario
Examine the differences between an LLC and a C-corporation.

## Asistente
An LLC and a C-corporation are two different legal structures used for businesses. 

LLCs are a type of pass-through entity, meaning that the owners of the business are taxed on the profits of the business without the business itself being taxed. LLCs also offer pass-through liability protection: the owners of the LLC are not personally liable for the debts and other risks of the business. 

C-corporations, on the other hand, are separate legal entities that are taxed on their own profits. Their shareholders are generally taxed separately on any profits they receive from the C-corporation. C-corporations offer greater flexibility when it comes to issuing stock and issuing dividends to shareholders. They also may be eligible for better tax rates. However, they come with double taxation: the corporation and the shareholders are both taxed on their profits.
