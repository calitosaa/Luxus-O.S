Compare and contrast blockchain and distributed ledger technology.

## Asistente
Blockchain and distributed ledger technology (DLT) are both based on the idea of a peer-to-peer, decentralized network of computers. The primary difference between them is that a blockchain allows for the transfer and storage of digital tokens and other digital assets, while distributed ledger technology allows users to transfer and store digital records. Blockchain also has built-in features such as smart contracts, which allow users to automate various processes. In contrast, distributed ledger technology is more of a general purpose platform that allows users to store and transfer any kind of digital data. Both technologies offer enhanced security and transparency, but blockchain is the more established technology and is better suited to digital assets, while distributed ledger technology is more suitable for tracking data and securely sharing information.
