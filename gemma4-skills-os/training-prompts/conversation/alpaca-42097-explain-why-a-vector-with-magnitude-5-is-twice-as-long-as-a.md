## Asistente

That is, a vector with a magnitude of 10 will be twice as long as a vector with a magnitude of 5. In other words, when two vectors have different magnitudes, the vector with a higher magnitude will always be longer than the vector with a lower magnitude. This is because the magnitude of a vector determines how much “length” it has. Therefore, a vector with magnitude 5 is always going to be half as long as a vector with magnitude 10.
