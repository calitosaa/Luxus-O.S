## Asistente
A data-driven society implies that data is collected from across multiple sources, from individuals to organizations, to automate and improve decision-making processes. This can make processes more accurate, efficient and timely, and also allows for better decision making by providing real-time insights about trends and current events. It also has implications for privacy, by exposing individuals and organizations to greater levels of surveillance and data collection. The implications are not just limited to business; data-driven societies can allow governments to make more informed decisions, while also making it easier to implement policy initiatives. However, there is a risk of data being abused to manipulate individuals or lead to biased decision-making, so steps need to be taken to protect personal data and ensure ethical practices.
