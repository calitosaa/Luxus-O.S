## Asistente
Aristotle and Plato both believed that ethical behavior is essential for achieving a good life, but their views on what constituted good action differed significantly. Aristotle's view emphasized practical wisdom and was more concerned with actions in particular situations, whereas Plato's approach focused more on developing virtues and knowledge of the ideal Forms. Aristotle placed greater emphasis on reason and the logical implications of actions, while Plato held that virtues such as justice and courage should be the foundation of ethics.
