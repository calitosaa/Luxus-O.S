Explain the science behind air pressure.

## Asistente
Air pressure is a measure of the force of the air particles in a given area. It is determined by the number of air molecules in the atmosphere, and varies depending on the altitude, temperature, and humidity of the environment. As air particles move, they create pressure and pressure gradients, which are areas of increasing and decreasing pressure. Because air is a gas, the particles can easily expand and contract. This is why a tire, for example, can become flat due to a decrease in air pressure. Air pressure is also responsible for the formation of clouds, as the increasing pressure forces heavier droplets of water toward the ground. Thus, air pressure is integral in understanding the phenomenon of our atmosphere.
