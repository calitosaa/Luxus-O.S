## Asistente
A Gini coefficient is a measure of inequality that is commonly used in economics. It is a number between 0 and 1, where 0 is perfect equality and 1 is perfect inequality. The Gini coefficient is calculated by measuring the area between the Lorenz curve and the line of perfect equality, which represents the cumulative share of the population held by each income group.
