## Asistente
Socrates and Aristotle were two of the most influential ancient Greek philosophers. Socrates was focused on the exploration of moral values, reasoning, and the nature of justice. He often used a dialogue-based method of teaching, which focused on the questions rather than absolute conclusions. Aristotle, on the other hand, favored an understanding of reality based on observation, experimentation, and reasoning. His topics of study ranged from scientific inquiry to political theory. Both philosophers believed in the power of reason—but while Socrates looked for right answers through dialogue, Aristotle believed that empirical evidence was the foundation of knowledge.
