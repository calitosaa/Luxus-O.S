Decision trees and random forests are both supervised learning algorithms that are used in a variety of tasks, such as regression and classification. The main difference lies in how they make predictions. Decision trees use a series of if-then statements to determine how to classify a sample data point, while random forests use an ensemble of decision trees. The result of using random forests is generally a more accurate model than what is produced by a decision tree. Random forests also require less tuning than decision trees since they don't require the same level of combination-selection process used for decision trees.
