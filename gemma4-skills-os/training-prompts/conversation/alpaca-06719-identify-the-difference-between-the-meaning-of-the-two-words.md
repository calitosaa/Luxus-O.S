## Asistente
The primary difference between the words 'impose' and 'impress' is that 'impose' means to force or coerce somebody to do something while 'impress' means to make an impact on somebody, either positively or negatively.

For example, you can 'impose' a law on the people in a country that forces them to follow it, while you can 'impress' someone with your intelligence, knowledge or some kind of performance.
