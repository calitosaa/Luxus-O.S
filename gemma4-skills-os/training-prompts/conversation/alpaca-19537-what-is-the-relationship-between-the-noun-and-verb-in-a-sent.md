## Asistente
The relationship between the noun and verb in a sentence is typically that of the subject and predicate. The verb expresses the action of the subject or depicts the state of being of the subject. In other words, the verb is the action word or predicate of the sentence that informs the reader what the noun or subject is doing or what is its state. For example, in the sentence "The dog chased the cat," the noun is "dog" and is the subject of the sentence, and the verb is "chased" and is the predicate of the sentence.
