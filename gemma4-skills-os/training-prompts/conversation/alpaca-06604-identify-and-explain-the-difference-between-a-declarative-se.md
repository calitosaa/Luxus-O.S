## Asistente
A declarative sentence is a statement that conveys information, typically containing a subject and a predicate. For example, "The sky is blue". A question, on the other hand, is a type of sentence used to ask for information. It typically starts with an auxiliary verb, such as "do" or "can", and the subject is usually not included. For example, "Is the sky blue?".
