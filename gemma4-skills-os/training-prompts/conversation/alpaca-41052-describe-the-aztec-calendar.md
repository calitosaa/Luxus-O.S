The Aztec calendar was an adaptation of the Mayan calendar, used by the Aztecs and other Pre-Columbian people in Central Mexico. It was a 365-day calendar that was divided into 18 months, each with 20 days. The remaining 5 days were considered unlucky and were not part of the regular year.
