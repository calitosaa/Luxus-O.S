Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) is a technique used for amplifying short segments of DNA to produce thousands to millions of copies of a particular DNA sequence. It is carried out in a cyclic manner through a number of temperature cycles, creating multiple copies of the target DNA sequences in a very rapid and cost-effective way.
