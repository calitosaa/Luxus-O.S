Compare and contrast the effects of a production-driven economy and a service-driven economy

## Asistente
A production-driven economy is based on the production and manufacture of physical goods which are then distributed to consumers. This type of economy is characterized by its extensive use of physical capital and production methods. The production-driven economy is focused on tangible goods and making profits from the sale of these goods. On the other hand, a service-driven economy places emphasis on the provision of services as a main source of economic activity. This type of economy places emphasis on intangible activities such as software development, consulting and entertainment services. The service-driven economy is focused on providing value to customers through service offerings, and making profits through providing these services.
