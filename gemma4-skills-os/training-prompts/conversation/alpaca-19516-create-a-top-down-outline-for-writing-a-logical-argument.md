## Asistente
A top-down outline for writing a logical argument includes the following: 
1. Introduction: provide a clear statement of the position or argument to be summarized. 
2. Body: provide evidence, facts, and figures to support the argument.
3. Premises: present the premises upon which the argument is based and provide reasoning to explain the relationship between the premises and the conclusion. 
4. Objections: anticipate and address potential counter-arguments and objections, refuting them with evidence.
5. Conclusion: restate the argument and summarize the main points of the outline.
