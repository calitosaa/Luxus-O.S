## Asistente
The OODA loop is a decision-making process developed by US Air Force Colonel, John Boyd. It stands for observe, orient, decide, and act and is also known as the Boyd Cycle. It emphasizes the importance of understanding the environment and being able to make decisions quickly in order to gain an advantage over opponents. Observation is the process of gathering and analyzing information about the environment. Orienting involves using the information to understand the situation and identify the potential opportunities and threats. Deciding is the process of selecting the course of action. Finally, acting is the process of implementing the course of action and adjusting based on the feedback from the environment.
