## Asistente
Impressionism and Expressionism are both artistic styles, but they differ in their approach to art. Impressionism is a style that emphasizes the artist's individual perception of a scene, focusing on light and color to capture a fleeting moment in time. Expressionism, on the other hand, is a more emotionally driven style that focuses on the artist's inner feelings and emotions, often resulting in paintings with chaotic, distorted, and lively compositions. While both styles can be used to capture the artist's subjective interpretation of their environment, Impressionism is more focused on the artist's representation of what they physically see, and Expressionism focuses on the artist's emotional or spiritual interpretation.
