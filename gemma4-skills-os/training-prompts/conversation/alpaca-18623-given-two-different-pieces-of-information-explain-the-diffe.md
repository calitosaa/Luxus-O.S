## Asistente
An ecosystem is a community of living organisms and the environment they affect. It can contain biotic (living) components such as plants, animals, and microorganisms, as well as abiotic (nonliving) components such as water, atmosphere, and soil. A biome is a large group of ecosystems that share the same climate and have similar types of plants and animals. An example of a biome is a tropical rainforest, which contains several unique ecosystems within it.
