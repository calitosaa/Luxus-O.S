## Asistente
A UX (User Experience) Designer is responsible for researching, designing, and developing user experiences that are useful, engaging, and easy to use. They create wireframes, develop prototypes, and conduct user research and testing to ensure that the product meets the needs of users. This is done by studying design trends and feedback, developing a good understanding of user behavior, and studying the competitive landscape. UX Designers are also responsible for creating user flows, journey maps, and other design artifacts that help product teams understand user needs, validate design decisions, and make informed decisions about product direction and development.
