The three different types of sonnets are the Italian sonnet, the Shakespearean sonnet, and the Spenserian sonnet. The Italian sonnets are composed of an OCTAVE (eight lines) and a SESTET (six lines), with the rhyme scheme ABBAABBA CDECDE or ABBAABBA CDCDCD. The Shakespearean sonnets are composed of three QUATRAINS (four lines) and a COUPLET (two lines) with the rhyme scheme ABABCDCDEFEFGG. The Spenserian sonnets are composed of three QUATRAINS and a COUPLET with the rhyme scheme ABABCBCCDCDEE.
