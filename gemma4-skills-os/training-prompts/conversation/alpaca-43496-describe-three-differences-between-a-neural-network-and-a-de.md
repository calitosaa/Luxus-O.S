Describe three differences between a neural network and a decision tree

## Asistente
Three differences between a neural network and a decision tree are:
- Neural networks can learn and make educated guesses, while decision trees rely on predefined rules and don't have the capacity to learn.
- Neural networks are most often used for complex tasks such as image or speech recognition, while decision trees are used for simpler tasks such as classification.
- Neural networks can be trained with large amounts of data, while decision trees require less data for training.
