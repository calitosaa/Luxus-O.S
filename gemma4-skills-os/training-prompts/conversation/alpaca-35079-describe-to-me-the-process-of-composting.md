Composting is the process of breaking down organic materials into nutrient-rich soil. The process involves mixing equal parts of green materials – such as grass clippings, vegetable scraps, and coffee grounds – with brown materials – such as leaves and straw – and moistening the mixture with water. The mixture is then aerated to increase the oxygen supply and covered with a layer of soil or straw to help hold in heat and moisture.
