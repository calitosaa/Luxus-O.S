## Asistente
A public good is a commodity or service that is available to everyone and cannot be restricted or blocked by individuals or organizations. It also doesn’t get “used up” when accessed, meaning it can be consumed by everyone at the same time. A classical example of a public good is national defense - the security it provides is non-excludable and non-rivalrous, which means it can be enjoyed by everyone without fear of exclusion and without being “used up” by others. Other examples of public goods include streetlights, healthcare, education, and many of the services provided by the government.
