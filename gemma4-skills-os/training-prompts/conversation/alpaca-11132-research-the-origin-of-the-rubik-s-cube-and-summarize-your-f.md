The Rubik's Cube was invented in 1974 by Ernö Rubik, a Hungarian professor and sculptor. His goal was to create a three-dimensional puzzle that would illustrate three-dimensional geometry. He originally called the puzzle the 'Magic Cube', but it was later changed to the Rubik's cube. The cube was first introduced to the public in 1980 and quickly became a worldwide phenomenon.
