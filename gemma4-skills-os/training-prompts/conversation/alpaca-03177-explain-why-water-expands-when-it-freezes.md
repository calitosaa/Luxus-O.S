## Asistente
When water freezes, the molecules arrange themselves into crystalline structures. This process is accompanied by an increase in volume, causing the ice to expand as a result. As the water molecules move further apart with each additional layer of the crystalline structure, the amount of expansion increases. This increase in volume is known as the principle of expansions. The higher the temperature of the water, the more the crystalline structure is able to form, and the greater the expansion.
