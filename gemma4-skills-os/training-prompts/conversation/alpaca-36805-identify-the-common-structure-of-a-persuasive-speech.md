## Asistente
The common structure of a persuasive speech typically includes an introduction, definition of the issue and position, body, conclusion, and call to action. The introduction sets up the topic, providing context and introducing the speaker’s position. The body is the main portion of the speech, which includes evidence, facts, real-world examples, and stories to support the speaker’s position. The conclusion reinforces the main points, summing up the arguments and leaving the audience with a clear call to action.
