Climate change is the long-term shift in average weather patterns and is caused by the buildup of greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide in the atmosphere. This buildup can cause an increase in the average global temperature, leading to various impacts such as rising sea levels, melting glaciers, more frequent and intense droughts and heat waves, harsher weather patterns, and increased spread of diseases. Human activities such as burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial emissions are one of the main causes of climate change. The emissions of carbon dioxide, methane, and other greenhouse gases trap heat and energy in the atmosphere, leading to warming temperatures. Deforestation, a process of removing forests to clear land for agricultural activities and other land uses, reduces the amount of carbon dioxide that the trees can absorb, thereby increasing the amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere. Another human activity that contributes to climate change is the production of industrial products and burning of fossil fuels, which release carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. To reduce climate change, individuals and businesses can reduce their emissions by making changes such as switching to renewable energy sources, using energy efficient appliances, and adopting sustainable farming practices.
