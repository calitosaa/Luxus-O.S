## Asistente
IPv4 and IPv6 are the two most commonly used protocols for Internet-based communication. IPv4 is the fourth version of the Internet Protocol and has a 32-bit address space, allowing for roughly 4.3 billion unique IP addresses. IPv6, on the other hand, is the sixth version of the Internet Protocol and features a 128-bit address space, allowing for far more potential connections. IPv6 is designed to be more secure and efficient than IPv4, with better support for mobile Internet and enhanced end-to-end encryption. IPv6 also allows providers to host more websites, as the larger address space provides more potential IPs. IPv4 remains in use due to its widespread adoption, but IPv6 is quickly gaining ground due to its improved features.
