An Ethernet cable consists of four pairs of copper wires covered in insulation. The wires are twisted in pairs in order to reduce interference, and the Ethernet cable itself is further protected by a plastic sheath. At each end of the cable there is a connector which is used to plug the cable into devices. The most common connector is an RJ-45 connector.
