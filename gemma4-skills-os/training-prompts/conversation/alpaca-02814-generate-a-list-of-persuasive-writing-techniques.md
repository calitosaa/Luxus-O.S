## Asistente
- Eloquent Language: The use of strong, descriptive and emotive language to evoke an emotional response.
- Evidence: Making use of facts, statistics and examples to provide proof of your argument.
- Repetition: Repeating key phrases, ideas and words to reinforce your argument and make a lasting impression.
- Anecdote: Using stories and anecdotes to bring abstract ideas to life and engage the reader.
- Appeal to Authority: Leveraging expert opinion, research or citations to support your claims.
- Contrast: Highlighting differences between two or more things to illustrate a point.
- Loaded Language: Using emotionally charged language to influence and persuade the reader.
- Metaphor/Simile: Making use of vivid metaphors and similes to illustrate a point and appeal to the senses.
- Rhetorical Question: Asking a question that the reader can’t answer but encourages them to think in a certain way.
- Alliteration: Using words with the same start to create a catchy, memorable message.
