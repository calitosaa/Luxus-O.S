## Asistente
Microservices architecture is an approach to software development that separates applications and services into independent, loosely coupled components. This approach has several benefits, such as improved scalability, flexibility, and reliability. By breaking an application into individual components, it becomes easier to deploy, modify, and maintain the application. Additionally, the smaller, self-contained components of a microservices architecture can be developed, tested, and deployed independently, allowing for greater scalability, faster updates, and a more reliable application overall.
