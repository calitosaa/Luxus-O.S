## Asistente
The tri-state buffer is a type of digital logic circuit that uses three states rather than the two states used in traditional logic circuits. It is a circuit that has three possible states - high, low and high impedance or 'Z' state.
The high impedance state allows the tri-state logic signal to be connected to multiple devices sharing the same input or output line while not transferring data. Each bus line connected to the tri-state buffer can be driven 'high' or 'low', and when the tri-state buffer is in 'Z' state, none of the three other states is active, thus allowing the other devices to control the bus line.
