## Usuario
How does a monopolist maximize its profit?

## Asistente
A monopolist maximizes its profit by producing where marginal revenue equals marginal costs (MR=MC). Marginal revenue is the additional revenue a monopolist earns for one more unit of output, and marginal cost is the additional cost a monopolist incurs for one more unit of output. The monopolist will set its price at a level where the quantity demanded is equal to the quantity supplied, resulting in maximum profit.
