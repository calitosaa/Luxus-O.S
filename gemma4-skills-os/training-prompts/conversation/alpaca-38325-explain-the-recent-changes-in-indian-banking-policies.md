The financial inclusion program seeks to increase access to banking services and encourage low-income people to open bank accounts.

The government is also introducing a number of initiatives to increase bank credit and reduce the incidence of financial exclusion including:

• Allowing banks to refinance retail and SME loans.
• Establishing Credit Guarantee Fund Trusts for MSMEs by the Government of India to provide guarantees for loans up to Rs 5 crore.
• Offering tax incentives to encourage banks to lend more to the MSME sector. 
• Reduced Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements for opening bank accounts.
• Strengthening consumer protections through amendments to the Banking Regulation Act, 1949.
• Implementing a Unified Payments Interface (UPI), a real-time payments system for customers to transfer money electronically. 

These changes are designed to improve access to banking services across India, reduce financial exclusion and promote growth in the banking sector.
