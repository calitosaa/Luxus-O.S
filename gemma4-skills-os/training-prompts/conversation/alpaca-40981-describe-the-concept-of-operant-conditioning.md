## Usuario
Describe the concept of Operant Conditioning

## Asistente
Operant conditioning is a form of learning in which either positive or negative reinforcement is used to modify an organism’s behavior. It is based on the idea that behavior is shaped by its consequence, either reinforced or punished. Reinforcement involves providing a reward for desired behavior, while punishment involves some form of penalty for undesired behavior. In operant conditioning, the environment influences behavior, and the behavior influences the environment. This feedback loop helps to shape future behavior, either positively or negatively, thereby allowing organisms to adapt to their environment.
