## Asistente
The main difference between cash and accrual accounting systems is the timing of when transactions are recorded. In a cash accounting system, transactions are only recorded when they are paid, while in an accrual accounting system, transactions are recorded when they occur. This means that cash accounting is more immediate and focuses on the actual amount of money changing hands, while accrual accounting requires more forecasting and attempts to account for future transactions. Additionally, accrual accounting often produces more detailed reports and more accurate financial predictions.
