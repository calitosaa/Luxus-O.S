The American Civil War was fought primarily between the Northern and Southern states from 1861 to 1865 over the issue of states’ rights, an issue that had been in dispute since the Constitution was ratified. By popular opinion in the South, these rights included the right to keep and extend slavery. Northern states disagreed and tensions led to secession and the firing on Fort Sumter in 1861.
