How did the discoveries of Copernicus challenge the traditional view of the universe?

## Asistente
The discoveries of Copernicus challenged the traditional view of the universe because he proposed that the Sun, not the Earth, was at the center of the universe. This was in contrast to the Ptolemaic view of the universe that had the Earth at the center, and with the Sun and other planets revolving around it. Copernicus' model also shifted the notion of the heavens as perfect, instead stating that the stars and planets could be imperfect and influenced by gravity and other forces.
